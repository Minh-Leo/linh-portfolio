Deakin Spark Deakin Spark

I started to learn and do research about entrepreneurship since I was in high school, but it was rather an alone journey. Whenever an idea comes into my mind (which is a lot of times, sometimes in the same day), I would write it down and save it there. Till this point, I currently have a huge collection of ideas, yet never had an opportunity to initiate or execute them.

Coming to the SPARK Deakin Ideation Program with a beginner’s mind, I hope to learn new things and step-by-step techniques to bring any good ideas into life.
Other than that, I hope to build a network of like-minded pals who have the same interests and energy to create new products or services that make an impact. I made this blog series to record my learning journey throughout the program, and am excited to see how much I grow afterwards.

Soonta .com.au Soonta .com.au Soonta .com.au

At Soonta, we are passionate about our food. We strive to provide healthy and hearty Vietnamese fusion food to savvy Adelaide eaters. Our menu is inspired by the ancient rule of Vietnamese cuisine - the balance between Ying and Yang. Or, the balance between meat and vegetable, spiciness and freshness, traditional Vietnamese foods and a modern Australian palate, and, most importantly, the balance of nutrition. Our salads, bread rolls and food bowls are freshly prepared in our kitchen and served every day. A bit of kindness and a delicious meal goes a long way!

SOMEwhere

Moving from places to places has been a pleasure for me ever since I grew old enough to travel by myself. A box of flight ticket collection, a passport full of visa stamps, and millions of stories of thousands of miles across North America, Asia, Oceania and a tiny bit of Europe have gradually defined a part of my identity among my surroundings - friends and family. Of course, the stories I brought back usually relate fun, surprising, the-awe-moment and happy memories that would inspire others to pursue the same lifestyles as me. Of course, there were moments of solitude, of tiredness, of culture shock, of embarrassment and tons of other many real-life things that would turn away all the excitement. However, I (we) tend to remember the signature, the new discovery, the “highlight” of it all after the trip.
