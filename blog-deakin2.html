The _In-between_
Moments of Doubt and   [New Ideas]

3 August 2020

I guess most entrepreneurs must experience these kinds of moments - the “in-between” moments of doubt and when new ideas actually show up. For the past week, I learned a lot from the workshop and from my own research. However, after making different problem trees and the value proposition model, and also discussing ideas with my friends, I had this feeling that my idea is too small to address such big issues out there. Those big issues that I suggested myself either require a big funding scheme, a huge switch in policy, or influence such an established retail system. The more I did research, the more I became doubtful about my plan. That was also when Melbourne’s stage 4 lockdown announcement came on the news.



Then, I took the weekend off for myself and did some self-reflection, made some video calls with my long-distance family members and reconnected with some old friends as their birthdays are coming up. That was when I realized that human interaction is extremely important and in turbulent situations like right now, all we need (however, we not all the time realize) is to talk to someone, keep the connection going on, and create new traditions with our loved ones. Traditions like going out for coffee, brunch or exploring new things are being limited right now, but it doesn’t mean we can come up with new ideas and have fun with each other.



When reconnected with some of my friends, I also got to know that their businesses are in bad places right now. The products are not in a good selling point, and marketing must temporarily stop because of the low budget.

That was when I decided to come up with a business idea that can address both issues above. An online gift box shop that you can use to send/exchange gift boxes with different themes for your friends and family. The idea is written in more detail below.

Imagine you and your friends received the same box of goodies with new different brands/products (body-care, or weird snacks, new brands of coffee) and you can try it together and give feedback through those video calls. Instead of talking about how bad COVID is or how bored it is at home, you can now try new things, make jokes and laugh together. This idea, I hope, will bring more value to people’s lives at the moment. Personally, I always love receiving parcels or postcards in the mail and it always makes my day, I hope this will bring joy to others as well.

The business will provide different types of curated boxes to meet different demands. The suppliers will be small local businesses that need more sales during difficult times. This can also benefit them in terms of increasing brand awareness in the community. One of the most important tasks for me at the moment is to find more small businesses and discuss with them about this idea as well as the budget and transaction process. Also, I must do more research on finding suppliers that provide eco-friendly, recycling packaging and boxes. Although this will be a long and solo journey, I can’t wait until everything unfolds.

Prototyping

10/08/2020

This week’s session is about Prototyping!

Personally, I found this session very interesting and insightful because I neither have much knowledge nor experience in prototyping. I did draw out some UX workflow on before to visualize the website design and function. However, I never use any digital platform to demonstrate it.

My business idea requires both physical and digital prototypes. Physical prototype involves designing packaging shape, locate items in boxes and decorate following different themes. Digital prototype involves setting up an online website for customers to select their cart and make purchases. This week I focused on the digital side!

I was introduced to Marvel and MockFlow - very cool platforms for simple prototyping. It works like Wordpress or Squarespace, having all necessary elements in order for business owners to develop their sites and design as well as implement some UI techniques. Because it is simple, it doesn’t allow me to customize some elements.

Later on, I found out about Figma. Figma is great! As I am used to using Photoshop and other Adobe softwares, I like the way Figma allows me to customize everything from scratch. I prefer Figma for its flexibility and for its scale system.

So, after a few attempts to prototype my business on Figma last week, I kinda developed some better ideas to add in the big picture to make my products niche compared to those in the market. I guess when the website came into its official look and function, I got to see what is missing in the puzzle and also realize there is more work into this than I ever thought, especially with designing a good experience for customers when they are in my store digitally.

Business Model

24/08/2020

This week’s session is about Business Model Canvas. This is week 5 already!!, which means it is half way to Pitch Day and we are reaching the business side of our projects.

The first activity was to customize our own elevator pitches. Elevator pitch should be short, cut to the chase, while showcasing the special point in the business and answer the “Why would anyone pay attention to our products?”. I found this task is necessary and important and quite easy, well, if I talk to a friend and tell him/her about what I do, but giving me 20 seconds to give a stranger the whole picture of my whole business idea can be intimidating. My strategy was listing down some important keywords that would directly describe the products I offer, the imagery and feelings my store brings, and what the meaning behind all that.

“I am the founder of Happy Goodies, an e-commerce store that customizes gifts and delivers monthly mysterious boxes to those that subscribe to the service. This service helps people regain positive energy, reconnect with their loved ones and save some shopping time. Happy Goodies’ suppliers are local small businesses that have suffered from COVID-19 lockdown who are in need to increase revenue and make sales.” #supportlocals

This is the first draft I made for an elevator pitch. I am quite satisfied with it and it made the 20s cut; however, it does not tell a story in my opinion. I want my store to have a story that sticks in people’s minds. Something genuine and fun, yet unique!

So I made the second one, that includes my story in there!

“If you ever watched the movie called “You’ve Got Mail”, you will see how interesting the way everything has changed. The movie was in 1993 and everyone expressed absolute happiness and pleasure when receiving an email. 20 years later, nobody is that happy receiving emails, more or less we’re glad to get rid of it (well, unless it’s a job offer... or a postal tracking code.. or some good deals you may never have time to look at), but we are thrilled to hear someone knock on our front door, and hand over a package of goodies or a postcard from oversea. The trend is coming back, just like how vintage takes over fashion. Gallup Poll said 94% of people nowadays love getting physical mail and finding personal letters addressed to them among all of the bills and junk newsletters. This is the reason I created Happy Goodies, to bring happiness to you, and help you reconnect with your loved ones!

On a larger scale, Happy Goodies is created to help small businesses raise awareness, make sales, and stand strong during COVID period. We connect with many local businesses to bring the local goodies to your doors with customized designs and themes for different occasions. #supportlocalbusinesses”

Well, there it is! I probably need to buy them a coffee or something in order for them to listen to all this (hah) but yes this is the feeling I want to give out. I also probably gotta redo another version of my pitch and throw in some niche elements of the business to make it stand out.

Oki, moving on!

Next step is to create a business model canvas that briefly determines elements such as key partners, key activities, key resources, value propositions, customer relationships, channels, customer segments. This feels like a business report but in a brief table form.
